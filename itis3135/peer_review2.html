Evaluation

  • Submission / file setup: The home page loads correctly and navigation is easy to find at the top of the page.
  • Contrast & readability: White background with dark pink text is easy to read. The darker header bar and buttons stand out well against the lighter pink sides.
  • Font usage: Fonts are consistent across the logo/title, navigation, and main text. The heading font for "Sophie's Cakeshop" feels on-theme for a bakery.
  • CRAP Principles:
    • Contrast: Strong contrast between the deep pink header and the light pink side panels. Buttons also pop nicely against the white content area.
    • Repetition: Navigation styling, colors, and button shapes are repeated across the page, which keeps the design consistent.
    • Alignment: The heading, intro text, and cake image are well centered, giving the site a clean and organized look.
    • Proximity: The welcome heading, dotted divider, and intro paragraph are grouped together, clearly forming one section.
  • Header / main / footer: The page clearly has a header with nav, a main content section, and (if present) a footer. Make sure the same structure appears on every page.
  • Branding / h1: "Sophie's Cakeshop" in the header works well as the site/brand name. Be sure it is coded as an <h1> somewhere for semantics and accessibility.
  • Main heading (h2): "Welcome to Sophie's Cakeshop!" is a strong h2 for the home page and sets the tone for the site.
  • Tagline / description: The sentence about custom, made-to-order cakes with unique Asian flavors describes the business clearly and concisely.
  • Navigation: Home, About, Menu, Gallery, and Contact are clearly visible in the header. Navigation layout is easy to use and matches the style of the rest of the site.
  • Content pages: Looks like there are multiple content pages (About, Menu, Gallery, Contact). Make sure each has enough bakery-specific content and consistent layout.
  • Images: Hero cake image is appealing and on theme. Check that all images in the gallery (when complete) have descriptive alt text, like "slice of matcha cake with fruit topping".
  • Menu: On the Menu page, listing specific cake flavors, sizes, and price ranges would give customers more useful info.
  • Gallery: A gallery with several different cakes (weddings, birthdays, etc.) will really help show variety and quality.
  • Contact / inquiry: The "Start a Custom Order Inquiry" button is great. Be sure the contact or order form collects helpful details (occasion, date, flavor, size, budget).
  • Responsiveness: Layout appears centered and should adapt fairly well, but make sure to test on mobile so that the header, intro text, and buttons don't feel cramped on smaller screens.
  • Stop: Stop leaving any placeholder text on inner pages; replace it with real menu items, gallery captions, and contact details.
  • Start: Start adding more personality to the copy (for example: signature flavors, story behind the shop, and pickup/delivery info). Also consider adding hover effects to the buttons to make them feel more interactive.
  • Continue: Continue using the cohesive pink color palette and centered layout. It fits the bakery theme very well and makes the site feel warm and welcoming.
